ADAPT

Actively Detecting APTs


ADAPT is a project that aims at automating the detection of an APT, and if detected, classify which APT the threat resembles.

To detect an APT, we believe a statistical model that analyzes the frequency of various ATT&CK techniques observed within a network in conjunction with known APT behavior (from MITREâ€™s Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) repository) can help determine if an APT is in a network, and possibly, what APT is indicative of that behavior.

What is an APT?

An advanced persistent threat (APT) is a set of stealthy and continuous computer hacking processes, often orchestrated by a person or persons targeting a specific entity... APT processes require a high degree of covertness over a long period of time.
